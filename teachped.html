Teaching Pedagogy &
Philosophy

Pedagogy: the method and practice of teaching, especially as an academic subject or theoretical concept.

Philosophy: a theory or attitude that acts as a guiding principle for behaviour.

Overview

As a TAS teacher, my philosophy revovles around creating a student-centred, engaging learning environment, be that in a workshop, kitchen, ag-plot, textiles lab or a conventional classroom. I prioritise the safety and individual needs of each student, encouraging active participation and exploration. Through my explicit teaching, I aim to provide clear, structured instructions to enable students to build upon strong foundational knowledge in theoretical and practical capacities.

Feedback

Feedback is essential in my classroom, due to the largely practical-based elements of the Course Content I cover, if students are performing a skill incorrectly, it may prove to be hazardous, and therefore regular, ongoing discussions will be had that provide students opportunities to learn, and correct techniques. It's sole intention is the guiding of students towards success, through safe and achievable methods.

Safety

In the TAS KLA, safety is paramount. I am committed to creating a safe and supportive environment both physically and emotionally, where students can learn, explore and experiment with confidence in themselves, and me. Many hazards will arise in the Course, but with the proper foundational understandings, I will have faith in my students to respond correctly, and also in my own knowledge to prevent or reduce the likelihood of danger.

Explicit teaching

Whilst I will continue championing a student-centred approach, I recognise the imporance of explicitly teaching technical skills, allowing for clear and concise instruction. By demonstrating and breaking down complex concepts and skills into simpler steps, I will ensure that each student can actively participate throughout each scaffolded step or procedure.

Lifelong Learning

I am a firm believer that despite your age and situation, you need to be continually learning to fully thrive. I model this by constantly adding to my craft, learning new methods, theories or skills to employ in my classrooms, or to pass onto my students. I encourage my students to see learning as an ongoing, lifelong pursuit. I also do my best to create positive environments for my students to help instill a love for and of learning. TAS is a complex KLA, and to navigate the ever-changing nature of technology, an adaptable and open mind is needed.
